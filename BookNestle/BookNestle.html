OUR CATEGORIES



ENGLISH BOOKS

"To Kill a Mockingbird"

Author:Harper Lee

Short Description:Set in the racially charged atmosphere of the American South during the 1930s, this Pulitzer Prize-winning novel explores themes of justice and moral growth through the eyes of young Scout Finch. Her father, Atticus Finch, is a lawyer defending a Black man falsely accused of raping a white woman.

1984

Author:George Orwell

Short Description:A dystopian classic, "1984" delves into a totalitarian society governed by the oppressive regime of Big Brother. Orwell's chilling portrayal of a world where privacy is nonexistent and truth is manipulated serves as a powerful critique of surveillance and authoritarianism.

Pride and Prejudice

Author:Jane Austen

Short Description:This beloved novel follows the romantic entanglements and social maneuvers of Elizabeth Bennet and Mr. Darcy in early 19th-century England. Austen's sharp wit and keen observations of social class and marriage make this a timeless exploration of love and societal expectations.



TAMIL BOOKS

Piraḻamal

Author:Jeyamohan

Short Description:This contemporary novel by Jeyamohan explores the complex lives of characters caught in the intersecting currents of modernity and tradition. With its rich narrative and deep character studies, it offers a poignant reflection on the human condition.

Porrudselvan

Author:Kalki Krishnamurthy

Short Description: A historical novel set in the Chola dynasty, "Porrudselvan" is a captivating tale of adventure, politics, and romance. Kalki Krishnamurthy's epic narrative brings to life the grandeur and intrigue of medieval Tamil Nadu through its heroic protagonist, Arulmozhivarman.

Ezhuththalar

Author:Ashokamitran

Short Description:This collection of short stories by Ashokamitran offers a window into the everyday lives of ordinary people in Chennai. His sharp observations and engaging storytelling highlight the nuances of human relationships and urban life.



JAPANESE BOOKS

Norwegian Wood

Author:Haruki Murakami

Short Description: This novel explores themes of love, loss, and mental health through the eyes of a young man, Toru Watanabe, in 1960s Tokyo. Murakami's evocative storytelling paints a poignant picture of the complexities of relationships and personal growth.

Snow Country

Author:Yasunari Kawabata

Short Description:Set in a remote hot spring town, this classic novel follows the turbulent affair between a wealthy man and a geisha. Kawabata's lyrical prose and exploration of human loneliness and beauty earned him the Nobel Prize in Literature.

No Longer Human

Author:Osamu Dazai

Short Description:This semi-autobiographical novel delves into the life of a man who struggles with alienation and a profound sense of disconnection from society. Dazai’s raw and introspective narrative captures the essence of existential despair and personal failure.



KOREAN BOOKS

The Vegetarian

Author:Han Kang

Short Description:This highly acclaimed novel tells the story of Yeong-hye, a woman who decides to stop eating meat, leading to dramatic changes in her life and those around her. Han Kang’s exploration of family dynamics and personal autonomy is both haunting and thought-provoking.

Kim Ji-young, Born 1982

Author:Cho Nam-joo

Short Description:This impactful novel examines the life of Kim Ji-young, an ordinary woman born in 1982, and the systemic gender inequalities she faces throughout her life. Cho Nam-joo's work is a powerful critique of modern Korean society’s gender roles and expectations.

The Happy Foreigners

Author:Kim Young-ha

Short Description:In this novel, Kim Young-ha delves into the lives of foreigners living in Korea, capturing their struggles and triumphs as they navigate cultural and personal challenges. The book offers a unique perspective on the experiences of expatriates and their search for identity in a foreign land.
